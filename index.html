zendo.mk/admin

↑ The storefront is live at zendo.mk — but the real engineering lives behind the login. This is a working simulation of the admin console I built: orders streaming in live, per-vendor commission rates, supplier balances, payout runs and role-based access. Ship an order in Orders, pay the vendor in Payouts, then switch the role to Vendor and watch RBAC scope everything down.

Zendo.mk is not a side project — it’s my company. I founded it and run it as a multi-vendor marketplace in production for the North Macedonian market, and I built the entire platform myself: from the first Prisma schema to the deployed product, covering the customer storefront, the vendor & admin dashboards, and the financial engine underneath it all.

Customers browse a full product catalog, add to cart, check out and track orders. Each seller company gets its own role-gated dashboard to manage products, orders, workflows and reporting, while the platform admin oversees everything — users, companies, permissions, commissions and payouts.

The hardest part wasn’t the UI — it was the money. I designed the commission calculation, supplier balances, unpaid-order tracking and payout history logic so that every denar flowing through the marketplace is accounted for. The console above simulates exactly that engine — watch orders stream in, deliver one, then run the payout and follow every denar.
